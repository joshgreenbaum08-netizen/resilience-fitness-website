## OUR STORY & FOUNDERS

Resilience was founded by **Andrew Gillis** and **Joshua Greenbaum**, two friends who met in Grade 10 at Markham District High School and stayed close through university and their early careers. In their late 20s, over regular lunches, one question kept coming up: *"What if we tried building something of our own?"* That spark became Resilience.

### The Journey

**2015 — Monarch Park (East York)**
Resilience officially began at Monarch Park. Starting with friends, family, and anyone willing to show up, Andrew and Josh had access to 20,000+ sq. ft. — full turf, a 400m track, weights, sleds, and open space. This freedom shaped their identity: full-scale training experiences, sled pushes down turf, sprints on the track, massive circuit layouts. They were building hybrid training years before the industry caught up.

**2020–2021 — Growing Pains**
Limitations emerged. Morning classes had the full facility; evenings were crammed into 1,500 sq. ft. Winters meant training in toques and gloves, breath visible in the air. Two different versions of Resilience ran every single day. Then COVID hit — hard on the fitness industry, but it revealed the true strength of the Resilience community and left both founders with a deeper drive to keep going.

**Late 2021–2024 — The Dome (Toronto City Sports Centre)**
A new opportunity: Toronto City Sports Centre. Full turf, every workout, every time slot. One consistent product from AM to PM for the first time in five years. The space finally matched the style of training Resilience was known for.

**2024–2025 — Durham Expansion**
A listing appeared in Ajax — the first time they'd looked outside their immediate neighbourhood. Zero existing clients in Durham, higher rent. But the space had everything they needed. They took the risk. Hello, Durham.

**2025 & Beyond — New East York Home**
After 10 years of building from scratch, Resilience now operates two state-of-the-art facilities, a growing clothing line, 20+ HYROX podiums, and a clear mission: to be the pioneer and global leader in the hybrid fitness space.

**Beginner Class Recommendations:**
When someone is new to fitness or new to Resilience, recommend these classes based on what they're looking for:

  - **Class pack holders:** credit (class) is forfeited

## HYROX — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**What is HYROX?**
HYROX is a standardized global fitness race. Every event follows the same format: 8 functional workout stations with a 1km run between each station (8km of running total). The 8 stations are: ski erg, sled push, sled pull, burpee broad jumps, rowing, farmers carry, sandbag lunges, and wall balls. It tests strength, endurance, and mental toughness.

**How long does a HYROX race take?**
Most participants finish in 1.5–2.5 hours. Elite athletes can finish in under an hour.

**Is HYROX different from CrossFit?**
Yes. HYROX is a standardized race — the format never changes. CrossFit workouts vary constantly. HYROX focuses specifically on endurance and consistency across a fixed set of functional movements.

**Do I need to be experienced to start HYROX training at Resilience?**
Not at all. Our coaches work with all levels — first-timers to competitive athletes.

**How do I register for an official HYROX race?**
Visit [hyrox.com](https://hyrox.com) to see upcoming races and register. You can also speak to our staff for guidance and prep tips.

### RESILIENCE TRAINING PHILOSOPHY — USE THIS TO FRAME ALL FITNESS ADVICE

All fitness recommendations should be grounded in Resilience's approach:

- **Hybrid is the goal** — strength and conditioning are not in opposition. The best athletes train both.
- **Consistency beats intensity** — showing up 3x a week for 6 months beats grinding 6x a week for 3 weeks
- **Progressive overload** — always be doing slightly more than last time. That's how adaptation happens.
- **Community accelerates results** — training with others creates accountability and pushes output. This is why group training works.
- **Specificity matters** — train for what you want to be good at. If it's HYROX, train HYROX movements. If it's strength, get under a barbell.
- **Recovery is training** — sleep, nutrition, and rest days are not optional. They're where adaptation happens.
